myODEON App Remake

Introduction

Odeon Cinemas is a cinema brand mainly operating in the United Kingdom. Whilst I was working as a Cinema Experience Manager at one of their cinemas in London, the company was in the middle of launching a new app - myOdeon. I helped the team fetch ideas about how to improve its functionality. Since I was physically present at the cinema I had feedback about the new app from the customers themselves.

My Role

UX/UI design

Problem

The major issue with the app was the location of the cinema ticket that the customer was struggling to find since it was only located in the account section of the app. On top of that, the customer had to have an account created already or create one on spot in order to access their digitally purchased ticket. This was inefficient, for instance, when the customers were running late for the screening. One particular cinema was especially an issue, where the customers had to show the ticket multiple times during their visit (i.e. at the entrance to the cinema, when ordering food, at the entrance to the screen).

Goal

Improve the cinema ticket visibility in the app

Impact

A significant reduction in customers' frustration that effected the whole cinema experience and a significant reduction of customers' time spent looking for their ticket in the app.

Ideation

The original app screen did not contain a sticky navbar, only a menu that was collapsible on the left-hand side. Therefore, I created a navbar that would always be present at the bottom of the user's screen. Then, I suggested the ticket be "always-at-hand" by placing it in the form of a QR code in the middle of the navbar. This way, the customer would have an access to it anytime he or she was asked to show their ticket.
